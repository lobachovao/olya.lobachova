Case study · Execue · SaaS

Fixing the onboarding gap that was costing activation

01 · The problem

Users couldn't launch

30+ client accounts were struggling to get their first campaign live. Three patterns kept recurring in every support session:

  • 1Unclear messaging in cold outreach — users didn't know what good copy looked like
  • 2Difficulty structuring multi-step campaign flows
  • 3Slow, confusing setup inside Instantly and HeyReach

Result: delayed launches, high support volume, and frustrated users who questioned the product's value before ever seeing it work.

02 · My approach

Research, then systems

I mapped the friction points first, then built reusable systems to address each cluster:

  • 1Direct user interviews and support session analysis to surface root causes
  • 2Built a scorecard framework to evaluate cold outreach message quality
  • 3Designed ICP filtering logic and AI-assisted copy templates to remove the blank-page problem
  • 4Documented 15+ recurring product bugs and UX gaps as a structured backlog
  • 5Ran experiments with AI-assisted message generation to reduce copywriting dependency
03 · What I learned

It was a product decision problem

The onboarding gap wasn't a documentation issue — users needed opinionated defaults built into the product, not another help article.

The scorecard framework became the foundation for a repeatable onboarding process adopted across all new accounts. The product bug backlog gave the founding team a prioritised, user-validated list to act on immediately.

Key insight: the fastest way to improve product quality at an early-stage startup is to be in direct, structured conversation with every user.
